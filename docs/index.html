My Story

Growing up my mom always told me “You know... everything is a business right? You should learn business”. It was with these words whispering in the back of my mind when I decided to change the course of my college studies from jazz guitar to business. I subsequently graduated from DePaul University in Chicago with a double major in Finance and Economics and a double minor in International Business and Spanish.

I spent five years working in the financial services industry, where I loved the complex work and the demanding work environments typically full of type A individuals. However, it wasn’t long after starting in the industry that I noticed two things: first, the ability to translate human logic into computer code was quickly destroying and creating jobs; and second, many businesses use extremely antiquated software.

It was with this knowledge that I realized that learning to code would inevitably be my next step. After working as a senior financial analyst at Ayco, Goldman Sachs for three years, and soaking in as much knowledge as I could, I decided to take a leap. In January of 2020, I moved to Berlin, Germany to complete a 9 week full stack coding bootcamp at Le Wagon. It was during this bootcamp that I discovered that not only do I have a passion for the complexity of backend development, but that I really enjoy the creativity that comes with frontend development as well.

Currently, I am looking for work as a developer, and once again, my mother’s words are in my head: “Everything is a business”. Keeping this in mind, I am looking for a company with interesting problems where my background in finance can be used synergistically to benefit the business.

When I am not coding, you might find me producing/performing electronic music under the artist alias Amin Chavez, or reading. Here are a few books that changed me for the better:

  • • Small Is Beautiful by E. F. Schumacher
  • • How to Change Your Mind by Michael Pollan
  • • Liar's Poker by Michael Lewis
